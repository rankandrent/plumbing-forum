Common Shower Plumbing Issue: Low Water Pressure

December 11, 2024
Is your shower water pressure not packing the punch you'd like it to? Low water pressure in showers is a common plumbing issue that most homeowners face at some point. A low pressure water stream can make your showering experience less satisfactory and could extend the time it takes for a comfortable, thorough rinse.

There are several reasons why your water pressure might be lacking. It could be due to pipe corrosion, a clogged showerhead, or even issues with the water main or the water regulator. Old plumbing systems often become corroded over time, causing obstructions that affect water flow. Simultaneously, mineral deposits from hard water can block your showerhead, reducing water pressure. If your neighborhood or city scales down the water pressure, you may also notice a decrease in your home.

I've tried a few things to address this issue. First, I checked and cleaned the showerhead for any mineral build-up. When that didn't work, I considered the age of my home and the possibility of pipe corrosion, so I had a professional inspect my plumbing system. While I found some minor corrosion, it wasn't substantial enough to drastically affect water pressure, leading me to consult my local water supply company. I recommend undergoing these steps if you are also experiencing this problem. Keep in mind that a thorough professional inspection can always provide clearer insight into your situation.

3. Check all faucets: It's also essential to check all of your faucets, not just your shower. If all taps have low pressure, the problem may lie with your main water supply.
